# Stat 256: Causality

**Instructor:** Chad Hazlett ([chazlett@ucla.edu](mailto:chazlett@ucla.edu))
**Quarter:** Spring 2026
**Class:** Monday, Wednesday, 11:00--12:15, Haines A44
**Department:** Statistics, UCLA

This course covers causality as it applies to the challenges of real applied research, with fluency in both the potential outcomes and structural causal model / DAG frameworks. Topics include identification strategies (experiments, selection on observables, difference-in-differences, instrumental variables, regression discontinuity), sensitivity analysis and partial identification, and advanced topics such as mediation, interference, and time-varying treatments.
